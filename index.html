Mobility — every session
Each move to the FIRST hint of discomfort. Hold 15–30 sec, 2–3 times.
Forward reach
Lie on your back; raise the arm overhead with your good hand or a band assisting.
External rotation benchmark
Elbow tucked at your side, band anchored at waist height; rotate the forearm outward. Have this one measured monthly.
Cross-body
Pull the arm across your chest with the other hand until you feel a firm stretch behind the shoulder.
Behind-the-back
Towel over the opposite shoulder; use the good hand to draw the affected hand gently up your spine.
Strength — once a day only
Controlled tempo, stay pain-free. 10–15 reps each.
Band external rotation
Elbow at side; rotate forearm out against the band, return slowly.
Band internal rotation
Elbow at side; pull forearm across your stomach against the band, return slowly.
Band row
Anchor band in front at chest height; pull elbows back, squeeze shoulder blades, release slowly.

Key Rules

  • Stretch to discomfort, never through sharp or radiating pain.
  • 24-hour rule: worse for more than a day, or worse night pain → ease off next session.
  • Flare-up? Ice 10–15 min and drop the next session's intensity.
  • Sleep with a pillow supporting the arm so it doesn't hang or get trapped under you.
  • Lifting & chin-ups are fine at controlled tempo — skip full-speed reps.
  • Re-check your irritability level with your PT every few weeks.
